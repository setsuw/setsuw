I absolutely hate how AI has been dominating tech recently. The readmes, the programming, all of it. If I check your project and I see a readme that was obviously AI generated, I'm not taking you seriously because you showed me that you have given up your voice and likely your code to an AI.

I don't care if you can barely write in English, I don't care if your code runs like crap, because at least you're trying. And when you try, you learn and improve, and that's something that people haven't been wanting to do since like 2020. I don't only blame AI, I also blame the way people have been using social media and the world wide web.

Forget the being on social media all the time, the problem isn't exposure alone. The problem is that people contribute to social media less and less (which, in the context of the mainstream ones, is a good thing lol), because I genuinely think the worst way to use social media is to just consume. Like if you're just scrolling or just liking and retweeting, you're using it completely wrong. You should always be putting your voice out there.

My partner asked me around a week ago why I comment so damn much on Tiktok, and now at the time I said "i dunno" because I didn't wanna sound like a nerd but the reason is because that's the right way to use social media. The point is to socialize and to make yourself heard and to make connections. There's absolutely no way I would have come into contact with the people I did if all I did when I sat on my ass all day was laugh, like, and scroll. I met the people I did, who inspired me and encouraged me to take the path I'm taking, by getting my voice heard. By posting my remarks, jokes, opinions, and arguments. Sure, most of them no one ever saw, but it's because of what people did see that made it worth it.

It's just that, the truth is, you won't find lasting connections by using AI to make your profiles and repositories "professional", you'll blend in with everyone else. I completely expect that some people will find my pieces of literature confusing to read, I know I word things weird sometimes, maybe there's a typo somewhere, and I'm sure I'll find this embarrassing in a year or two somehow. And you know what? I'm happy about that, because I know it's something that was made by a human and not a large language model. I know that this piece of work conveys the emotions I was feeling when I wrote it, and that it's something I genuinely agree with.

If you ask me, I think LLMs are fine for looking for inspiration or explaining concepts that you just don't understand. I don't think you should be using AI to write things for you or judge work that you made. You should be able to write about a project you made and you should be able to explain what your code does and why you wrote it that way. If you want help with your work, look to an online forum or a person you can rely on.

P.S. I would switch to decentralized social medias if I were you. If you use Twitter, switch to Plemora. Reddit goes to Lemmy, Instagram to Pixelfed, etc. You can learn more about the Fediverse here https://fmhy.net/social-media-tools#fediverse-tools
